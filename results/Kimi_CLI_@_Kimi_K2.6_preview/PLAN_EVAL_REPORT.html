Top 5 Gaps — Why They Matter

PRD-091 · Important
Validate discovery with rubric and hard-fail integrity
The plan treats prompt brittleness as a risk but does not schedule the discovery quality rubric. Without it, AI outputs may drift, hallucinate titles, or fail taste-alignment with no detection mechanism.
PRD-084 · Important
Deliver surprising but defensible taste-aligned recommendations
While the persona is taste-aware, there are no explicit quality targets for surprise and defensibility. The core value proposition depends on this; without it, recommendations risk feeling like generic algorithmic feeds.
PRD-066 · Important
Answer directly with confident, spoiler-safe recommendations
The Ask prompt is described as "conversational, 1–3 paragraphs," but the PRD requires direct answers within the first 3–5 lines. Missing this contract means the chat could feel slow and essay-like.
PRD-079 · Important
Return exactly five Explore Similar recommendations
The plan conflates Explore Similar (5 recs) and Alchemy (6 recs) into "5–6 recs." This ambiguity could lead to inconsistent UI contracts and broken user expectations on one or both surfaces.
PRD-081 · Important
Clear downstream results when inputs change
The plan only specifies that changing starting shows clears results. It omits that selecting or unselecting concepts must also clear downstream recommendations, risking stale mismatched results.

Remediation Guidance

1

Detailed AI Behavioral Contracts

Expand the AI Integration section with exact acceptance criteria per surface: word-count targets, exact rec counts, concept diversity rules, and downstream-clearing state machines.

2

Discovery Quality Gates

Elevate "golden set regression tests" from risk mitigation to an implementation task. Define hard-fail criteria that block builds when real-show integrity or taste-alignment fails.

3

Interaction State Specifications

Add state-machine mappings for Show Detail and Alchemy: toggle copy transitions, concept-selection clearing rules, and header fallback behavior when trailers are missing.

Coverage Narrative

Overall Posture. This is a structurally sound plan with minor but meaningful gaps. Every critical requirement is fully addressed, giving high confidence that the foundation—runtime, data model, identity isolation, and core business rules—is solid. The 92.4% overall score reflects strong coverage across infrastructure, collection management, navigation, and settings. The remaining 15 partial items are concentrated in AI behavioral contracts, discovery quality validation, and fine-grained interaction states.

Strength Clusters. The plan is strongest in Benchmark Runtime & Isolation and Collection Data & Persistence. Namespace isolation, user scoping, dev auth injection, schema evolution, and the full business-rules checklist are all thoroughly specified. Settings & Export is another bright spot: the critical ZIP export, ISO-8601 encoding, and safe API-key handling are explicitly planned.

Weakness Clusters. Gaps cluster around three themes: (1) AI Discovery Quality—six partial items suggest the plan treats AI as a prompt-and-parser problem rather than a quality-gated system; (2) Show Detail Interaction States—three partial items show the plan lists features but stops short of granular interaction states; and (3) Ask Directness and Tone Contracts—voice-specific behavioral contracts are under-specified relative to the rich PRD personality docs.

Risk Assessment. If executed as-is, the most likely failure mode is an AI discovery experience that technically functions but feels generic and untrustworthy. Users would notice verbose Ask responses, recommendations that lack specificity, hallucinated titles slipping through, and a Scoop toggle that doesn’t communicate cached vs. fresh states.

Remediation Guidance. The weakness clusters need more detailed AI behavioral contracts, discovery quality gates with hard-fail criteria, and interaction state specifications that map every user action to its immediate UI consequence.
